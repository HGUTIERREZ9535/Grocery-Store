# SALES ANALYSIS 
### Project Over view 
To conduct a comprehensive analysis of Blinkit's sales performance, customer satisfaction, and inventory distribution to identify key insights and opportunities for optimization using various KPIs.
### Data Sources 
The analysis is based on the "BlinkIT Grocery Data.csv" dataset,
which provides detailed information on grocery products, outlet characteristics, customer ratings, and sales performance.
### Tools
- Excel
- SQL Server Managment Studio 
### Data Cleaning 
In the initial data preparation phase, the following tasks were performed:
- Data Loading and Inspection 
- Handling Missing Values
- Data Cleaning and Formatting
### Exploratory Data Analysis
Based on the dataset analysis, the following key questions will be answered:
- Total Sales: The overall revenue generated from all items sold.
- Average Sales: The average revenue per sale.
- Number of Items: The total count of different items sold.
- Average Rating: The average customer rating for items sold.

### Results / Findings 
- Out of the 16 different types of items sold at the store, the highest sales come from fruits and vegetables, snack foods, household items, frozen foods, and dairy products.
- Seafood has the lowest sales in the entire store, generating $9.08 in sales with a total of 64 items sold.
- Tier 1 outlet locations need improvement to increase overall sales performance.
- Total sales over the years show a generally consistent trend, with no significant peaks or sharp declines except in 2011.
### Recomendations 
- Seafood items need to be promoted to increase sales through marketing campaigns, special promotions throughout the week, and in-store advertising.
- Target products that had the lowest sales in previous years and promote them on a larger scale. Since different types of products are purchased during specific seasons throughout the year, it is important to order more of the products that are in higher demand and less of those that are not needed at the time. This approach helps prevent products from spoiling and reduces losses for the company.
